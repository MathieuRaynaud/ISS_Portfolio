Analyze of the place of sport in the french society

Context

For the course of big data, by groups of two students, the objective was to choose a subject, find open data sets related to it, analyze the data using an open-source programming language, and conclude with the extracted elements.
I worked with Alex Noize for this course, and we chose to analyze the place of sport in the french society.

Tools and resources

To determine the place of sport in the french society, we tried to see if the investment, the life expectancy, the migratory tendencies were impacted by the number of sportive equipment per inhabitant.
To do so, we analyzed four open data sets, two from the french government (data.gouv.fr) and two from the french national institute of statistic and economic studies (INSEE).
Finally, we analyzed these data sets using the programming language R. You can access the GitHub repository in which we stored all the produced algorithms by clicking the button below:

Study of the Sigfox protocol

Context

For the course of "Protocols for the connected objects", and by pairs, all the students had to study a communication protocol between Sigfox, NB-IoT, LoRa, Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) and ZigBee.
Wael Ben Jemaa and me worked on Sigfox for this course. Our objective was to find some information about the protocol about its physical layer, its energy consumption, its mac layer and the security it implements.

Tools and resources

The hardest part of the work was to find relevant sources for the report. In fact, we can find a lot of information on the web, but we always have to check the truthfulness of it, and especially when we work on new technologies.
That's why we worked the most with official publications from Sigfox, as you can find on the website of the company.

Study of the Iridium Next constellation of satellites for telecommunications

Context

One of the objectives of the course of "Protocols for the connected objects" was to study the emerging telecommunication solutions for 5G communications.
As a consequence, with three of my colleagues (Wael Ben Jemaa, Jean-Baptiste Laffosse and Alex Noize) we chose to study the Thales Alenia Space Iridium Next constellation of satellites.
This constellation is composed by 81 satellites which will provide 5G communication to the whole world, at any point.

Tools and resources

To study the Iridium Next constellation, we mostly used the Thales Alenia Space website, which contains a lot of trusted information on the project. It allowed us to collect only relevant knowledge on the constellation.
