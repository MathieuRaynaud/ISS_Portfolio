Microcontrollers and Open-Source Hardware (MOSH)

For the course of Microcontrollers and Open-Source Hardware, our objective was to study and design a Printed Circuit Board (PCB). This PCB implements a 2-levels trans-impedance amplifier which gets 110nA in input and gives 1.1V in output.
This PCB was designed to be plugged on an Arduino Uno and to use the gas sensor we produced later.
We started by designing the trans-impedance circuit with an electrical scheme which is the following one:

    In the scheme above:
  • R5, at the entry, protects the operational amplifier against electrostatic discharges
  • R5 and C1 constitute a filter against voltage noise
  • C1 and R1 form a filter against current noise
  • R2 can be changed to adapt the calibre of the amplifier
  • C4 and R3 constitute an active filter
  • C2 and R6 filter the output
  • C3 filters the supply noise

Energy efficient future wireless communications

On December the 19th, 2018, I assisted to a conference given by profesor Nuno Norges Carvalho from the University of Aveiro, in Portugal. Profesor Nuno Borges Carvalho is IEEE Fellow and IEEE Distinguish Lecturer, and the topic of the conference he gaved was: "Energy efficient future wireless communications".
It was a really interesting conference in which he explained what will be the next technologies used in wireless communications.
Currently, the main problem in wireless communication is energy cosumption. In fact, we build smaller and smaller devices which are able to communicate with the Internet, but the reduction of the size of these devices leads to the reduction of the size of batteries they bring.
Consequently, future technologies to communicate will be technologies for which moving devices will consume very few resources or no resources at all to communicate, and will need to interact with a lot of fixed devices electrically supplied.

Merckathon of the Oceanological Research Center of Banyuls-sur-Mer

The Merckathon of Banyuls-sur-Mer is a Hackathon organized since 2017 by the oceanological research center of Banyuls and the INSA of Toulouse.
During two days, the 50 students of the INSA Innovative Smart Systems formation work on a subject they choose. The only direction is to create an innovative smart module useful for the research center.

During this Merckathon, I worked with four of my colleagues. We chose to deploy a module to plug on the divers of the research center, and able to automatically take some measurements in the sea during the dive. The objective was to make useful the time during which the divers are in the sea to take measurements and increase the quantity of data collected by the research center.

The solution we implemented used an Intel Edison with a temperature sensor, a pressure sensor, and an ultrasonic ranger sensor.

Here is a scheme which explains the way of working of our system:

    In the picture above, we can see several elements:
  • The diver, on the bottom, carries the smart module on his back which takes automatically measurements on the water at his position
  • The boat and the two buoys compute the position of the diver using ultrasounds waves and triangulation
  • The boat and the two buoys determine their own position by GPS to be able to give the GPS position of each measurement taken

On software side, I developed a dashboard using Node.js and the framework express to develop a web-oriented application. This one is able to extract the measurements done by the diver module from a CSV file, and display them on a Google Maps API. Each measurement is located by a pin at the relevant position which contains the information collected at this point.
